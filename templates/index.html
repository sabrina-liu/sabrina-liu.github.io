I began with pen-and-paper sketches to scope the problem space, map task flows, and identify must-have modules (route discovery, safety cues, and community tips). Low-fidelity wireframes reduced premature commitment and enabled rapid iteration.

I ran focus groups (n≈6) to surface user needs around confidence, route legibility, and local knowledge. Thematic coding highlighted clarity and perceived safety as primary decision criteria.

I translated insights into a clickable Figma prototype. Information architecture emphasised a prominent search, a legible route card, and a safety overlay toggle. Iterations targeted scannability and error prevention.

I compared two layouts for route discovery (map-first vs. list-first). Outcome metrics: click-through to route details, time-to-first-decision, and perceived confidence (7-point Likert). List-first improved decision speed; map-first improved perceived control.

The final BikeWithUs release integrates the winning elements: list-first discovery, immediate safety cues, and a streamlined route detail view with community notes. Iterative research demonstrably reduced cognitive load.
